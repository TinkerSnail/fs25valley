**Living town-life is this mod's central differentiator, and the community reaction to FS25's base NPCs
confirms the opportunity rather than undercutting it.** (Insight crystallized with the user 2026-06-29.)

**The community sentiment (researched 2026-06-29):** FS25 added voiced NPCs to make towns feel livelier, but
reception is divided-to-negative — complaints about voice acting, immersion ("don't want an NPC by my house
all day"), and a realism gap. The loudest organized response goes the OTHER way: a popular "Hide NPCs" mod +
forum threads like "delete from existence thy NPC's." A vocal segment wants the NPCs GONE.

**Why that's an OPPORTUNITY, not a warning (the user's framing):** players hide the NPCs because, from a
PLAYER's seat, they're unsalvageable — the only lever a player has is the on/off toggle (endure or remove).
But that's a player's toolset, not a MODDER's. Everything that makes the base NPCs feel hollow (stand in one
spot, generic lines, no schedule, no relationships, no daily life) is exactly the layer THIS MOD operates on:
we hook the same NPC type and drive real schedules/routes, characterized dialogue, homes they leave & return
to, social visiting, seasonal events. The community verdict isn't "NPCs are a bad idea" — it's "GIANTS'
*implementation* is bad and we can't fix it." We can. The thing players wrote off is precisely our surface.

**Design implications:**
- A *well-done* living town is a clear differentiator BECAUSE the base game fumbled it.
- But respect the "Hide NPCs" crowd: keep our ambient-life polished, non-intrusive (don't crowd the player's
  farmyard), and ideally TOGGLEABLE.
- Grounds the town-life TODO items: per-NPC town routes, social visiting / co-presence, business NPCs at
  doors, seasonal events/parades, and the per-character dialogue/backstory work.
